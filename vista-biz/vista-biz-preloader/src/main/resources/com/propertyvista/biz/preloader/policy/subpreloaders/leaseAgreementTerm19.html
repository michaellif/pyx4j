a. Unless specifically permitted in writing in advance by the Landlord, the Tenant must not keep or allow on the Residential Complex or the Premises, any pet, including a dog, cat, reptile or exotic animal, domestic or wild, fur bearing or otherwise.

 

b. Where the Landlord has given his permission in advance in writing, the Tenant must ensure that the pet does not disturb any person in the Residential Complex or neighbouring property, and further the Tenant must ensure that no damage occurs to the Premises or Residential Complex as a result of having or keeping the pet. This is a material term of this Agreement. If any damage occurs caused by the pet, the Tenant will be liable for such damage and will pay the Landlord sufficient monies to compensate the Landlord in respect of damages, expenses, legal fees, or any other reasonable costs incurred by the Landlord. Further, if the Landlord gives notice to the Tenant to correct any breach, and the Tenant fails to comply within a reasonable time, the Landlord has the right to end the tenancy along with making the appropriate claims against the Tenant.

 

c. Having regard to the potential noise factors, health requirement and mess, the Tenant will not encourage or feed wild birds or animals at or near the Residential Complex.

 

d. The Tenant shall indemnify and save the Landlord harmless from any claims arising from injury to any person or damage to any property as a result of the Tenant or his guests bringing any animal, bird, reptile or pet in or about the land or buildings where the Premises are situated.

 

e. The Tenant agrees to remove the animal, bird, reptile or pet from the Premises should any complaints be received from the Landlord concerning same. When using common areas of the Premises, the Tenant agrees to keep the animal, bird, reptile or pet on a leash at all times, to clean up after the animal, bird, reptile or pet and to muzzle if required by law.
