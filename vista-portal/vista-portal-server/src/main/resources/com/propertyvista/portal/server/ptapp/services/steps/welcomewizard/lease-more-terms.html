Delivery

If the Landlord is unable to provide possession of the Premises at the commencement of this Tenancy Agreement because a previous Tenant refused to leave, or for any other reason, the Landlord will not be responsible for failure to give the Tenant possession on that date; however, the Rent payable under this Tenancy Agreement will be adjusted to be payable from the date vacant possession of the Premises is provided.  The term of this Tenancy Agreement will not be extended but if the Premises is not provided to the Tenant within thirty (30) days from the commencement of this Agreement, either party may terminate this Agreement by giving the other notice in writing.

Parking

Any parking space(s) allocated to the Tenant by the Landlord may be used only for the purpose of parking one private passenger automobile, which is regularly operated by the Tenant.
The Tenant agrees not to allow guests to park in the designated tenant parking areas, without the written consent of the Landlord.
It is expressly understood and agreed that the Tenant shall not park or store on the Landlord’s property any additional automobile, any automobile which has been abandoned or is inoperable or does not bear any valid license permit.  Furthermore, that the Tenant shall not park or store on the Landlord’s property any commercial vehicle, recreational vehicle, trailer, boat or any other object.
If the Tenant permits any vehicle to be parked in a location other than the allotted parking space(s) or area, the Landlord shall be entitled to remove the vehicle(s) from the Property at the vehicle owner’s risk and expense.
The Tenant agrees not to obstruct driveways, entrances or exits on or about the Residential Complex.
No repairs, cleaning, washing or maintenance to any vehicle shall be carried out on the Landlord’s property.
Automobiles shall be parked only in spaces designated by the Landlord from time to time and the Landlord shall have the right to reassign the parking spaces at any time as the Landlord, in its sole discretion, may determine.
The Tenant shall provide the Landlord with information it may require to identify the Tenant’s automobile, including but not limited to make, model, year, colour, license plate, ownership and particulars of registration of the vehicle and the Tenant shall affix to the vehicle such identification as may be designated by the Landlord. The Landlord is not required to allot any specific parking space(s) until all requisite information is provided.
The Landlord shall have no obligation to provide parking space(s) for more than the number of automobiles specified in paragraph 3. 
The Tenant shall not assign or sublet any parking space(s). 
In the case that the Tenant wishes to terminate his parking privileges, the Tenant shall provide thirty (30) days notice in writing to the Landlord.  The Landlord will offer no guarantee that the same parking space will be available should the Tenant wish to reinstate their parking agreement at a later date.
If the Tenant is in contravention of any of the provisions of this paragraph, the Landlord shall have the right to remove the automobile, vehicle, trailer, boat or object, as the case may be, from the property of the Landlord at the Tenant’s risk and expense.  The Tenant shall take no legal proceedings of any kind against the Landlord or its agents as a consequence of such removal and the Tenant shall reimburse the Landlord for any expense which he may incur in removing, storing or disposing of any vehicle, trailer, boat or object.
It is understood and agreed that the Landlord, his servants and agents shall not be responsible for any damage, which may occur to the Tenant’s vehicle or its contents by any means whatsoever caused, including but not limited to damage, or loss by fire, theft, negligence, malicious damage or damage caused by the malfunction of any garage door.
If at any time during the tenancy the Tenant does not have a vehicle, there shall be no reduction in the Monthly Rent in respect of the parking space(s) and the Tenant agrees to return all access keys or cards to a secured parking area to the Landlord.

Abandonment of Premises by Tenant / liquidated damages

If rent is unpaid after the due date, and if it reasonably appears to the Landlord that the Tenant has vacated or abandoned the premises, the Landlord may enter the Premises and, in addition to all other rights reserved to the Landlord, may re-rent the Premises.
All The Tenant agrees to pay to the Landlord any costs incurred by the Landlord in respect of commission fees, advertising, administrative costs, storage fees, and all costs of redecorating and cleaning, in addition to any arrears of rent and damages, including but not limited to all legal costs on a solicitor and client basis, to which the Landlord is entitled under this Agreement or by law in the course of obtaining vacant possession and re-renting the Premises.  It is expressly agreed that recovery of any such sums shall constitute appropriate damages recoverable by the Landlord as a consequence of breach of this Agreement.
Upon re-entry by the Landlord following termination of the tenancy or following the Tenant’s abandonment or vacating of the Premises or pursuant to an order of a court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction, the Landlord may dispose of any articles, belongings, effects, or furnishings of any kind found at the Premises or its environs in accordance with the provisions of applicable provincial legislation.  Any property found in the premises shall be deemed to be the Tenant’s property unless the Tenant advises otherwise, in writing, prior to such re-entry.
