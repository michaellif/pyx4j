a. The Tenant shall exercise reasonable care and diligence in the use of any utility supplied by the Landlord.

 

b. Utilities that are not included in the rent or are not paid to the Landlord are the responsibility of the Tenant who must apply for hook up and must maintain current payment of the utility account. The discontinuation of utility service resulting from the Tenant’s cancellation or failure to maintain payment of the utility account is a breach of the material term of this Agreement. The Landlord has the right to end the tenancy if the Tenant fails to correct the breach within a reasonable time after receiving written notice to do so.

 

c. The Tenant acknowledges that payment of charges by the Tenant for services as outlined above constitutes a rental obligation of the Tenant and any unpaid charges may be collected as rent; furthermore, any money paid by the Tenant to the Landlord, even when specifically identified by the Tenant as “rent”, may first be applied by the Landlord to any unpaid charges for the above services, and the Landlord shall have the same remedies in respect of a resulting shortfall in rent as in the case of non-payment of rent.

 

d. The Tenant also acknowledges that where hydro is currently included in the rent, the Landlord, to effect energy conservation, may, at any time during this tenancy, choose to meter the Tenant’s Premises separately and transfer responsibility for payment of hydro directly to the Tenant based on the Tenant’s own consumption, upon thirty (30) days written notice to the Tenant. In such an event the Landlord shall reduce the Monthly Rent in accordance with any applicable rent control legislation and the Tenant hereby consents to such transfer of responsibility for payment of hydro.
