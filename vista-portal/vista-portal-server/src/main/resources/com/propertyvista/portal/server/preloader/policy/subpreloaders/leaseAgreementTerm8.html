a. Defects or Deficiencies: The Tenant hereby acknowledges that the Premises, appliances and fixtures are in clean condition free of visible defects and the Premises are fit for habitation and use. The Tenant undertakes to advise the Landlord, in writing, within seven (7) days of the commencement of the term of this Agreement, of any defects or deficiencies in the condition of the Premises.

 

b. Condition of Premises: The Tenant agrees to maintain the Premises in the same condition as existed at the outset of this Agreement, or as thereafter improved by the Landlord, reasonable wear and tear excepted. The Tenant agrees to keep the Premises in a reasonable state of cleanliness and shall be liable for the costs of cleaning the Premises and for the costs of repair of damage to the Premises or common areas caused by the willful or negligent conduct of the Tenant, other occupants of the Premises or persons permitted on the Landlord’s property or the Premises by the Tenant. If the carpet and blinds have been professionally cleaned prior to move-in by the Landlord, the Tenant shall be responsible to have them professionally cleaned upon move-out. If the Tenant fails to restore the Premises to the same condition as it was in on the date of commencement of this Tenancy Agreement, the Tenant will be liable for the costs incurred by the Landlord to do the same.

 

c. Maintenance Guarantee: Where the Tenant takes possession on a “back-to-back” move-in, the Landlord shall be permitted 30 days to enter the Premises for the purpose of effecting maintenance and repairs to the Premises and no abatement shall be demanded by the Tenant pending the Landlord’s completion of work hereunder during the aforementioned 30 day period.

 

d. Alteration, Remodeling, Decorating:

      i.        The Tenant will not make or cause any structural alteration to be made to the rental unit or residential property.

     ii.        The Tenant shall not alter, renovate or decorate the Premises without the Landlord’s prior written approval and shall, on or before termination of the tenancy, remove any alterations, renovations and decorating and restore the Premises to the same condition as it was in on the date of commencement of this Tenancy Agreement, reasonable wear and tear excepted.

    iii.        The Tenant agrees that there is no promise, representation or undertaking, by, or binding upon the Landlord, with respect to any alteration, remodeling, decorating or installation of equipment or fixtures in the Premises.

    iv.        The Tenant shall not permit the painting of any portion of the Premises, or erect or cause to be erected any structure, including but not limited to fences in, about or upon the Rented Premises or thing, which would result in holes being created in the Landlord’s structure, including but not limited to the installation of satellite dishes, in, about, or upon the Premises.

     v.        Wallpaper shall not be installed without prior written consent of the Landlord.

    vi.        Spikes, hooks, screws, nails, or stick-on hangers shall not be put into or upon any woodwork of the Premises. Hooks, nails, tapes or other devices for hanging pictures or plants or for affixing anything to the rental unit or residential property will be of a type approved by the Landlord and used only with the Landlord’s prior written consent.

   vii.        No adhesive products or self-adhesive products shall be used within the Premises, including, but not limited to, picture hangers, clothes hooks, refrigerator decorations and bathroom decals.

  viii.        Painting, papering or decorating of the rental unit or residential property will be done only with the Landlord’s prior written consent and with Landlord approved colours.

 

e. Electric Light Bulbs and Fuses: The Landlord shall provide electric light bulbs in the fixtures and fuses in any panel box installed by the Landlord at the time the Tenant takes possession of the Premises, but not during the term of tenancy. It is the Tenant’s responsibility to provide electric light bulbs not to exceed the recommended wattage requirement for the fixture and fuses not to exceed the recommended amp requirement, in the panel box. Upon termination of the tenancy, the Tenant shall ensure that all electric light bulbs and fuses are in place and in an operable condition.

 

f. Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors: The Tenant acknowledges receipt of maintenance information in support of any smoke detector and/or alarm and/or carbon monoxide detector (where applicable) and agrees to immediately notify the Landlord in writing of any damage to or malfunction of any fire safety equipment not limited to any smoke detector and or smoke alarm and or carbon monoxide detector (where applicable), supplied by the Landlord and the Landlord agrees to service same. The Landlord shall furnish, where applicable, a battery for each smoke detector at the time the Tenant takes possession of the Premises, which battery shall thereafter be replaced as needed from time to time by the Tenant. If a malfunction occurs due to the Tenant’s removal of or failure to replace the battery, or tampering or adjustments made thereto or removal thereof by the Tenant or his guests, the Tenant shall reimburse the Landlord for any expenses incurred for replacement or servicing of the equipment.

 

g. Mould and Mildew: The Tenant shall ensure the Premises are kept free of moulds and mildews by using appropriate products to clean fixtures and walls in high humidity areas such as washrooms, and by ensuring adequate ventilation to such areas by opening windows and interior doors to prevent excessive humidity in enclosed areas. The Tenant acknowledges liability and responsibility for any damage to the Premises caused by the Tenant’s failure to comply with this provision.

 

h. Fire: The Tenant shall not do, bring or keep anything in the Premises, or permit any act which will in any way create a risk of fire or increase the rate of fire insurance on the building or contents.

 

i. Window Coverings: The Tenant shall not install or permit to be installed over any windows or doors any flags, sheets, towels, metal, or other similar items which, in the sole opinion of the Landlord are detrimental to the appearance of the building. All drapes on exterior windows shall be white or shades of white so as to maintain a uniform appearance on the exterior of the Premises. When the Landlord supplies window coverings, the Tenant’s drapes and curtains may not be used without the Landlord’s prior written consent. Drapes and drapery tracks where provided by the Landlord shall not be removed.

 

j. Appliances: The Tenant may not install a washer, dryer, dishwasher, or similar equipment without the Landlord’s prior written consent. Any appliance or equipment supplied by the Landlord must not be repaired or removed without the Landlord’s prior written consent.
