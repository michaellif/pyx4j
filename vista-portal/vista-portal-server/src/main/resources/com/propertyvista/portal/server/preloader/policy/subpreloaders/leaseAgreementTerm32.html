a. If the Tenant desires to terminate the tenancy at the end of the term of this Agreement, he shall give WRITTEN notice in accordance with applicable law, which is as follows:

      i.        Alberta: NOT LESS THAN SIXTY (60) DAYS prior to the end of a tenancy year where the premises are leased under a yearly tenancy and NO LATER THAN the first day of any tenancy month where either the premises are leased on a monthly tenancy or it is after the expiration of the initial term of this Agreement, with such notice effective on the last day of the tenancy year or tenancy month, as the case may be.

     ii.        British Columbia: NOT LESS THAN THIRTY (30) DAYS prior to the expiration of this Agreement OR ANY STATUTORY OR OTHER RENEWALS THEREOF.

    iii.        Ontario: NOT LESS THAN SIXTY (60) DAYS prior to the expiration of this Agreement OR ANY STATUTORY OR OTHER RENEWALS THEREOF.

    iv.        New Brunswick: If the premises are let from year to year, at least three months before the expiration of any such year to be effective on the last day of that year. If the premises are let from month to month at least one month before the expiration of any such month to be effective on the last day of that month.

     v.        Nova Scotia: NOT LESS THAN THREE MONTHS prior to the expiration of this Agreement, where the Premises are leased from year to year and NOT LESS THAN ONE MONTH, where the Premises are leased from month to month.

 

b. A valid Notice of Termination of tenancy given by the Tenant shall be irrevocable upon receipt by the Landlord and shall not be withdrawn, rescinded or amended by the Tenant without the express written consent of the Landlord.

 

c. A valid Notice of Termination under sections (a) and (b) above must also:

      i.        identify the Premises for which the notice is given; and

     ii.        state the date on which the tenancy is to terminate.

 

d. The Tenant acknowledges that a notice of termination given by one Tenant is deemed to be notice given on behalf of all Tenants to this Agreement.

 

e. Any notice that fails to comply with section (c) above will be considered null and void and the Tenant will be required to provide a proper notice that complies with sections (a) through (c) above, failing which the Tenant will be required to pay to the Landlord any rent owed, any legal costs incurred enforcing the Landlord’s legal rights together with a sum to cover the Landlord’s out-of-pocket expenses, as determined from time to time by the Landlord at his sole discretion, for administration costs incurred with respect to the Tenant’s failure to provide proper notice to terminate a tenancy. This cost recovery does not include an amount payable by the Tenant as damages for a breach of this Agreement.

 

f. If either party has given notice of termination of this Agreement or if the parties have agreed that the tenancy will be terminated, the Premises may be shown to prospective Tenants in accordance with provincial legislation, as amended from time to time. Should the Tenant deny the Landlord entry rights between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm under the provincial legislation as amended from time to time or this Agreement, the Tenant shall compensate the Landlord for any damages and out of pocket expenses including but not limited to lost rent. Before entering, the Landlord will make a reasonable effort to inform the Tenant of the intention to do so.

 

g. The Tenant acknowledges and agrees that he shall give any Notice of Termination by delivering or mailing same at or to the office of the Landlord only, as set out on page 1, and not by delivering or mailing same to any agent or employee of the Landlord. The Tenant further acknowledges and agrees that, in the event the Landlord named in this Agreement ceases to be the Landlord of the Premises, the Tenant shall deliver any such notices under this Agreement or required by law to the office of the party who is the Landlord at the time the notice is given.

 

h. The Tenant agrees to vacate the Premises by 1:00 P.M. on the final day of this Tenancy Agreement or any renewal thereof.

 

i. After service of Notice of Termination, the Tenant shall arrange with the Landlord or his agent to complete a “Move Out Inspection Report”, which shall be signed by both parties. Failure to sign a “Move Out Inspection Report” will be deemed acceptance by the Tenant of the Landlord’s completion and accuracy of same.

 

j. Upon termination of the tenancy, the Tenant shall give vacant possession and deliver all keys of the Premises to the Landlord. Failure to comply with this provision shall render the Tenant liable to an administration charge in addition to any other liability imposed upon the Tenant by this Agreement or by law.

 

k. The Premises shall be left fit for immediate occupation by the new Tenant, clean, undamaged, and with all furniture and refuse removed. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Tenant shall:

      i.        leave the Premises, appliances, and appurtenances in the same condition as existed at the commencement of the term and if the condition of the Premises has been improved by the Landlord following the commencement of this Agreement in any manner or for any reason whatsoever, the Tenant shall leave the Premises in the said improved condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted;

     ii.        leave floors, walls, ceilings, windows, doors, and every other part of the Premises in a clean condition and not move heavy furniture over the floors or stairs without the use of coasters;

    iii.        leave the stove, refrigerator and any other appliance in a clean condition inside and outside, and replace any broken, missing or damaged parts before vacating;

    iv.        clean and defrost the refrigerator, but leave it running at normal setting;

     v.        professional clean carpet and fabric blinds;

    vi.        remove all contents and refuse from the Premises and leave any storage areas clean and unlocked;

   vii.        if the Tenant has or has had a pet, the Tenant at his cost shall have a qualified pest control contractor treat the Premises for fleas and other vermin and shall provide the Landlord with the contractor’s paid receipt as evidence of the work done; and

  viii.        should the Landlord have to clean or treat the Premises as a result of the Tenant’s failure to comply with his obligations, the Tenant shall reimburse the Landlord for all costs incurred in respect of same.

 

l. If this Tenancy Agreement is terminated pursuant to an order of a court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction:

      i.        the Landlord will not be liable for any loss of or damage to any personal property left in the Premises or the Residential Complex and if the Landlord removes such property, such removal is deemed to be approved by the Tenant; and

     ii.        the Landlord, in removing any personal property, will comply with all applicable laws, and will exercise care in the handling of the personal property to the fullest practical extent under the circumstances.
