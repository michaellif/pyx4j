a. Landlord’s Obligations

      i.        The Landlord must provide and maintain the Residential Complex in a reasonable state of repair, suitable for occupation by a Tenant.

     ii.        The Landlord must comply with health, safety and housing standards required by law.

 

b. Tenant’s Obligations

      i.        The Tenant must maintain reasonable health, cleanliness and sanitary standards throughout the Premises and the Residential Complex to which the Tenant has access.

     ii.        The Tenant covenants to advise the Landlord, immediately upon notice of the problem, in writing, of any repairs or maintenance required to be done by the Landlord. Any request for repairs or maintenance not made in writing shall not form the basis of any legal proceeding against the Landlord in a court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction.

    iii.        Further, upon written notice to the Landlord of any repairs or maintenance required to be done, the Tenant shall allow the Landlord a reasonable opportunity to effect repairs or maintenance that the Landlord is required to undertake by law or under this Agreement.

    iv.        The Tenant shall not call on any person not employed by the Landlord to make any repair or perform maintenance of the Premises and the Landlord shall not be responsible to compensate the Tenant if the Tenant chooses to do so. Further, the Tenant shall remain completely liable for any damage caused by any person contracted by the Tenant.

     v.        The Tenant must take the necessary steps to repair the damage to the Premises and/or Residential Complex caused by the actions or neglect of the Tenant or a person permitted on the Premises and/or Residential Complex by the Tenant.

    vi.        The Tenant is not responsible for repairs for reasonable and normal wear and tear to the Residential Complex. The Tenant shall be responsible for the cost of all repairs and replacements in the Premises including, without restricting the generality of the foregoing, broken glass, torn screens, broken locks and door trim, damaged light fixtures, plugged toilets and plugged sink drains. Repair requests must be reported immediately to the Landlord by the Tenant.

 

c. Emergency Repairs

      i.        Emergency repairs are limited to repairing major leaks in pipes or the roof, damaged or blocked water or sewer pipes or plumbing fixtures, the primary heating system, damaged or defective locks that give access to a rental unit, or the electrical system.

     ii.        If such emergency repairs are required, the Tenant shall immediately contact the Landlord’s 24 hour emergency helpline.

 

d. Accidents / Defects: The Tenant shall give the Landlord immediate written notice of any accident or defects in the Premises such as, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, defects or accidents involving water pipes and fixtures, gas pipes and fixtures, heating apparatus, tub surrounds, electric lights or any other installations and shall be liable for any damages caused by failure to give such immediate notice.

 

e. Electrical or Mechanical Systems: In the event of a breakdown of the electrical or mechanical systems, the Landlord shall not be liable for damages or personal discomfort; however, the Landlord shall carry out repairs with reasonable diligence.

 

f. Pest Control: The Tenant shall keep the Premises free from vermin and where the Tenant fails to do so, the Landlord or his agents, may enter the Premises for the purpose of exterminating any such vermin and where the Landlord deems the problem to be caused by the negligent behaviour of the Tenant, the costs thereof may be payable to the Landlord by the Tenant. The Tenant hereby further consents to entry of the Landlord or anyone designated by the Landlord for the purpose of treating the Premises for pest control purposes.

 

g. Appliances

      i.        Refrigerators - Ice shall not be scraped from any surface and electric defrosters or any type of electric appliance shall not be used in any refrigerator. The cost to repair any damage or to replace the refrigerator shall be paid for by the Tenant.

     ii.        The Tenant shall not use any appliance in addition to those supplied by the Landlord including, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, any dishwasher, air-conditioner, washing machine, clothes dryer, and refuse compactor, without first obtaining the written consent of the Landlord and paying to the Landlord the required charge for the use thereof.

    iii.        The Tenant shall properly care for all appliances supplied by the Landlord and notify the Landlord in writing if such appliances require repair; further, any damage to such appliances shall be paid for by the Tenant.
